## 1. How Do You Make Stylish Activewear?

### Design Principles for Premium Activewear

**Start with the silhouette, not the decoration.**
Premium activewear looks expensive because of cut, proportion, and fabric — not logos, prints, or bold colours. Think about what makes a plain black Lululemon legging look more expensive than a plain black Primark one. It's the fabric weight, the way it sits on the body, the seam placement, and the finish.

**Key Design Elements:**

1. **Seam architecture:** This is your biggest design tool. Where you place seams defines the silhouette, creates visual lines, and affects how the garment sculpts the body. Flatlock seams (4-needle, 6-thread) are the industry standard for activewear — they sit flat against the skin and prevent chafing. Strategic seam placement around the glutes can create lift and shape without any compression technology at all.

2. **Fabric weight and hand-feel:** This is what separates premium from high-street. Your leggings should be 200-280 GSM with a peached or buttery finish. When someone touches it, they should immediately feel the difference. Nylon-spandex blends (80/20 or 75/25) give the best balance of compression, stretch recovery, and luxurious feel.

3. **Minimal branding:** Your brand bible already nails this. The MM monogram is a "discovered" detail — inside the waistband, on a zip pull. Never a giant logo on the thigh. Premium activewear whispers.

4. **Colour palette:** Neutral/tonal colours perform best at premium price points. Sand, charcoal, espresso, cloud, stone. Add one seasonal accent colour per drop. Avoid neon, bright colours, or busy prints for your debut.

5. **Proportions:** High-waisted leggings (minimum 10-inch rise, ideally 11-12 inch). Cropped or slightly elongated tops. The jogger should be tapered but not skin-tight. The half-zip should hit at the hip, not the waist.

6. **Details that signal quality:**
   - Bonded hems instead of stitched (cleaner, more modern)
   - Tonal stitching (thread matches fabric exactly)
   - Hidden waistband pocket with clean zip closure
   - Branded zip pulls and hardware in matte black or matte nude
   - Interior printed care labels (not sewn-in tags)

### How the "Instagram Girl" Aesthetic Works

The brands that dominate Instagram activewear (Oner Active, AYBL, Bo+Tee, Alphalete) succeed because:
- **Flattering fit is everything.** The garment makes the body look good in photos. Sculpting, lifting, smoothing.
- **Colour coordination.** Matching sets photograph well. Always launch tops and bottoms in matching colourways.
- **Texture reads on camera.** Ribbed textures, subtle shine, matte vs sheen contrasts all photograph better than flat fabric.
- **Styling is built in.** A sports bra that doubles as a crop top. A half-zip that looks good open over a bralette.

### What Makes Maison Move Different
You're taking the Instagram-friendly silhouettes and sculpting BUT wrapping them in genuine technology and a luxury aesthetic. The difference is:
- Oner Active girl posts a gym selfie in matching sand leggings
- Maison Move girl posts a gym selfie in matching sand leggings AND then goes to brunch without changing, AND her muscles are recovering, AND the clothes still smell fresh

That's your story. That's the post. That's the caption.

## 2. How Is Activewear Printed?

### Printing Methods Ranked for Activewear

**1. Sublimation Printing (BEST for activewear)**
- Heat transforms dye-based ink into gas that bonds with synthetic fibres
- Works on polyester, nylon-spandex blends, and polymer-coated fabrics
- Result: vibrant, durable prints that won't crack, peel, or fade
- Ink is embedded IN the fabric, not sitting on top
- Perfect for all-over prints, gradient designs, subtle tonal patterns
- Survives repeated washing and high-stretch without degradation
- **Limitation:** Only works on synthetic/light-coloured fabrics

**2. DTF (Direct-to-Film) Printing**
- Prints design onto a special film, then heat-transfers to fabric
- Works on any fabric type and colour
- Good for small logos, limited branding details
- Less durable than sublimation for all-over prints
- More affordable for small runs

**3. Screen Printing**
- Pushes ink through a mesh stencil onto fabric
- Each colour requires a separate screen
- Best for simple logos, one-colour designs
- Very cost-effective at high volumes
- Less suitable for detailed or multi-colour activewear prints
- Can crack on stretch fabrics if not done correctly

**4. DTG (Direct-to-Garment)**
- Inkjet printer applies water-based ink directly to fabric
- Best for cotton and natural fibres
- Not ideal for synthetic activewear fabrics
- Great for one-off or very small runs
- Lower durability than sublimation on sportswear

### Recommended for Maison Move
- **For your debut collection:** You probably don't need printing. Your aesthetic is colour and texture, not print. Solid colourways in your neutral palette.
- **For interior branding:** Use heat-transfer labels (no sewn-in tags). Clean, premium, no irritation.
- **For future seasonal pieces:** Sublimation is your method if you ever introduce tonal prints or patterns. It won't compromise the fabric performance.
- **For the tech card insert:** Standard digital printing on 400gsm uncoated card.

## 3. Construction Methods

### Seamless Knit (for leggings and sports bras)
- Produced on circular knitting machines (Santoni, Lonati)
- No side seams — the garment is knitted as one piece
- Allows for zoned compression (different knit densities in different areas)
- More expensive than cut-and-sew
- Requires specialised manufacturers
- **Use for:** BodyMap Sculpt Legging, High-Support Sports Bra

### Cut-and-Sew (for everything else)
- Traditional method: fabric is cut from rolls and sewn together
- More design flexibility (pockets, zips, panels, mixed fabrics)
- Wider range of manufacturers can produce it
- Lower per-unit cost
- **Use for:** Jogger, Half-Zip, Lounge Set, Soft Lounge Legging, Lounge Bralette

### Flatlock Stitching (for all activewear pieces)
- ISO 607 standard for sportswear
- 4 needles, 6 threads
- Creates a flat, smooth seam that sits against the skin without chafing
- Essential for any piece worn during training
- **Non-negotiable for every Maison Move garment**

## 4. Tech Pack Checklist

For each style, your tech pack should include:

- [ ] Flat sketch (front, back, side)
- [ ] Measurements for every size (XS-XL)
- [ ] Fabric specification (composition, weight, finish, certification)
- [ ] Construction details (seam types, stitch count per inch)
- [ ] Compression zone mapping (for seamless pieces)
- [ ] Colourway specification (Pantone references)
- [ ] Trim details (labels, hangtags, elastics, zips, hardware)
- [ ] Branding placement (MM monogram position, size, method)
- [ ] Packaging specification
- [ ] Grading notes for size-specific adjustments
