# MaisonMove — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Brand Thesis

MaisonMove exists at an intersection that no one else occupies: **luxury-grade everyday activewear powered by clinically validated textile science.** The brand combines the aesthetic sensibility and community energy of brands like Oner Active with performance technologies — far infrared bioceramics, antimicrobial silver ion, phase change thermoregulation — that have never been unified into a single fashion-forward product line.

The target customer is the woman who exercises regularly but doesn't identify with gym-bro influencer culture. She wants activewear that performs, looks premium enough for coffee after a workout, and is backed by real science rather than marketing buzzwords. She is underserved by both the performance brands (too technical, too masculine) and the fashion-activewear brands (all vibes, no substance).

## The Vault Model

### What Is a Vault?

A Vault is a numbered, limited-edition product collection released as a timed drop. Each Vault is:

- **Numbered** — every unit carries a printed edition number (e.g., "047/250") on the inner label
- **Limited** — 250-500 units per Vault, sized to pre-validated demand
- **Final sale** — no returns, no exchanges (standard in limited-run drop culture)
- **DTC-only** — available exclusively through maisonmove.com
- **Technology-forward** — each Vault introduces or layers a new textile technology
- **Named** — Vaults carry collection names, not SKU numbers. They are chapters in a brand story, not inventory.

### Why Vaults (Not Traditional Launches)

| Problem | How Vaults Solve It |
|---|---|
| Cash flow risk on $10-20K capital | 250 units at ~£22 COGS = ~£5,500 total production cost. Fundable via pre-orders. One shot, contained risk. |
| Returns destroying margin | Final sale policy is accepted norm in limited-drop culture. Eliminates the 20-30% return rate that kills DTC apparel brands. |
| No brand recognition at launch | Scarcity creates perceived value. A 250-unit sellout generates more press, social proof, and FOMO than a 5,000-unit launch sitting at 40% sold. |
| Expensive fabric technology | Premium pricing (£89-95) is justified by exclusivity + tech. Customers pay more for something they might not be able to get. |
| Sizing uncertainty | Small runs let you collect real sizing data before committing to large production. Waitlist sign-ups include "What size do you typically wear?" |
| Manufacturer MOQ pressure | 250-500 units falls within the sweet spot for low-MOQ activewear manufacturers ($12-25/unit). No need for 1,000+ unit commitments. |

### Vault Naming Convention

Each Vault is named to create narrative momentum. The naming system reinforces collectibility and brand mythology.

```
VAULT 001 — "First Light"
VAULT 002 — "Silver Standard"
VAULT 003 — "Thermal"
VAULT 004 — "Meridian"
...
```

The numbering is permanent and sequential. There will never be another Vault 001. Buyers of early Vaults are founding members of the brand.

## Pre-Launch Phase (Months 1-3)

Everything before Vault 001 is about building the audience that makes the first drop succeed.

### Brand Foundation

- Register Ltd company (£50, Companies House)
- File UK trademark: Class 25 (clothing) + Class 35 (retail), £220 total
- Register domain (.com and .co.uk), Instagram, TikTok, X handles — all same day
- Celebrity partnership legal structure: entertainment lawyer, sweat equity agreement with vesting milestones (£2-5K, non-negotiable first spend)
- Product liability insurance (~£6-30/month)

### Content-First Brand Building

**Goal: 5,000+ email waitlist subscribers before spending a penny on product.**

The content strategy exploits MaisonMove's unique angle: the science behind the fabric. Most activewear brands sell aesthetics. MaisonMove sells "here's what's literally woven into this fabric and why" — and that educational content performs exceptionally well on TikTok and Instagram Reels.

**Content pillars:**

1. **"What's Actually In Your Leggings?"** — Myth-busting series. Compare standard activewear fabric to infrared-infused fabric under thermal cameras. Show what bioceramic minerals look like. Make invisible technology visible.

2. **"Building This Brand From Scratch"** — Behind-the-scenes journey content. Factory visits, fabric sample testing, wash tests, design decisions. People love watching the sausage get made. Transparency builds trust.

3. **Ruth-led content** — Comedy-meets-education. "Ruth Jones tries to understand what far infrared yarn is." Her audience (mainstream UK, 30-55, not gym culture) is exactly the underserved demographic. She makes the science approachable.

4. **Community challenges** — Pre-launch fitness challenges, run clubs, wellness content. Build the community identity before the product exists.

**Platform strategy:**
- TikTok: Primary growth engine. Educational + behind-the-scenes content. Post 4-5x/week.
- Instagram: Brand aesthetic, product teasers, community features. Post 3-4x/week.
- Email: Weekly updates to waitlist. Behind-the-scenes exclusives. Size preference surveys.

**Waitlist mechanics:**
- Single landing page (Carrd or Shopify coming-soon page)
- Email capture with: name, email, "What size do you typically wear?", "What do you look for most in activewear?" (data collection from day one)
- Referral program: "Move up the waitlist by sharing" (viral loop)

### Supplier & Technology Sourcing

Run in parallel with content creation:

- **CELLIANT outreach** — approach with brand deck, waitlist numbers, Ruth partnership. If rejected, activate Plan B (Emana yarn by Rhodia, direct bioceramic mineral suppliers, Baller Athletik supply chain research)
- **Manufacturer sourcing** — contact 3-5 low-MOQ manufacturers, request samples, negotiate pricing for 250-500 unit runs
- **Sample development** — minimum 2-3 revision cycles. Wash-test every sample 10+ times. Stretch-test under real workout conditions. QC inspection on 20% of any test batch.

## The Celebrity Strategy

### Positioning: Creative Partner, Not Spokesperson

Ruth Jones should never feel like a paid ad. The moment she does, both her audience and MaisonMove's credibility evaporate.

**The right framing:** Ruth is a co-creator. She has genuine input on design, naming, creative direction, and community strategy. She wears the product because she helped shape it, not because she's contractually obligated to post about it.

**Content approach:**
- She's funny, warm, and relatable — lean into that. "Ruth vs. the fabric scientist" content writes itself.
- She doesn't need to be in every post. She's the hook that draws people in. The brand and community sustain them.
- Her audience (mainstream UK women 30-55, Gavin & Stacey's 19.1M viewers) is the underserved market. She's the bridge between MaisonMove and millions of women who'd never follow a fitness influencer but would absolutely buy premium activewear from someone they trust.

**Audience value:**
- Instagram: 133K followers (direct reach)
- Cultural footprint: Gavin & Stacey finale — 19.1M viewers, most-watched UK Christmas broadcast in 23 years
- Press magnetism: Any product she's associated with gets coverage in mainstream UK media (not just fitness media)

**Legal structure:**
- Sweat equity with vesting over 24-36 months
- Clear deliverable milestones (X posts/quarter, X events/year, creative input sessions)
- IP assignment clause (anything she creates for the brand belongs to the brand)
- Minimum participation levels defined in writing
- Exit terms that protect both parties

### Stage 1: The Vault Era (Months 0-18)

**Objective:** Build an undeniable track record of sellouts, a loyal community, and a clear brand identity.

**Key metrics to hit before advancing:**
- 4-6 consecutive Vault sellouts
- 15,000+ email subscribers
- 50,000+ social media following
- Press coverage in 2-3 UK fashion/lifestyle outlets
- Cumulative revenue: £150-300K
- Repeat purchase rate: 30%+ of Vault 1 buyers returning for Vault 2/3

**Technology stack:** CELLIANT infrared bioceramic (Vault 001), add silver ion antimicrobial (Vault 002+)

**Community building:**
- Private community for Vault buyers (Discord, WhatsApp, or IG Close Friends)
- Behind-the-scenes access: fabric sourcing, factory visits, design decisions
- Ruth-hosted Q&As or live try-ons
- "Founding 250" identity for Vault 001 buyers — they're the origin story

**Revenue model:** 100% DTC, 75% gross margin, pre-order funded

---

### Stage 2: Controlled Expansion (Months 18-30)

**Objective:** Transition from "can't get it" to "hard to get it." Scale production while maintaining scarcity perception.

**Key moves:**
- Increase Vault sizes: 500 → 1,000 → 2,000 units
- Expand product line: leggings, bras, shorts, tanks, a jacket (6-8 SKUs)
- Fully integrate silver ion as standard across the line
- Launch IRL community activations:
  - Run clubs (start in one city, expand to 3-4)
  - Wellness mornings, yoga sessions
  - Vault buyer meetups
  - Brand partnership events (skincare, coffee, wellness brands)
- Attend trade shows: Pure London, Moda, Top Drawer (Selfridges buyers scout here)
- Build wholesale capability: production consistency, quality control systems, line sheets

**Do NOT approach Selfridges yet.** Build undeniable momentum first. They receive hundreds of pitches. You want them to already know who you are.

---

### Stage 3: The Wholesale-Ready Line (Months 24-36)

**Objective:** Create a separate premium product tier designed specifically for luxury retail, without cannibalising the DTC Vault model.

**The dual-tier model** (learned from Gymshark's "Everywear" strategy):

The DTC Vault line and the wholesale line are different products at different price points serving different purposes:

**DTC Vaults (ongoing):**
- £59-95 price range
- Numbered, limited, final sale
- Single or dual technology
- Available only on maisonmove.com
- The core community product

**Wholesale Capsule (new):**
- £95-165 price range
- Seasonal collections, not numbered
- Dual or triple technology (infrared + silver + premium fabrication)
- Exclusive to retail partners
- The prestige product

**Why separation is critical:**
- DTC community doesn't feel betrayed — their Vaults are still exclusive, numbered, unavailable in shops
- Selfridges gets something their customers can't buy online
- Higher wholesale price point absorbs the retailer margin structure
- Two distinct products, two distinct channels, zero conflict

**Wholesale pricing architecture:**

| | Retail Price | Wholesale Price (to Selfridges) | MaisonMove Margin | COGS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Legging | £145 | ~£58 (40% of retail) | 48% (~£28/unit) | ~£30 |
| Bra | £95 | ~£38 | 47% (~£18/unit) | ~£20 |
| Jacket | £195 | ~£78 | 49% (~£38/unit) | ~£40 |

48-49% wholesale margin is tight but viable. This is why the wholesale line must sit at a higher price point than the DTC Vaults. The premium fabric, dual technology, and Selfridges retail context justify the uplift.

**Critical rule:** DTC prices must always be lower than wholesale retail prices. DTC customers get the better deal (plus exclusivity). Selfridges customers pay a premium for the elevated product and the in-store experience. Both channels are happy.

### Stage 4: Retail Entry (Months 30-42)

**Objective:** Secure Selfridges placement. Start with a pop-up, convert to permanent.

**The pitch (by now you should have):**
- 6+ consecutive Vault sellouts
- 20,000+ email list, 75,000+ social following
- £300K-1M+ cumulative DTC revenue
- 40%+ repeat purchase rate
- Active community with IRL events in multiple cities
- Ruth Jones as creative partner (mainstream press credibility)
- The only activewear brand with clinically validated infrared + antimicrobial technology

**Entry strategy: Pop-up first**

Selfridges runs rotating pop-ups and activations constantly (The Corner Shop, Body Studio features, seasonal activations). A 2-4 week pop-up is:
- A lower-commitment yes from Selfridges (easier to get)
- A live test — if it sells, they'll invite you to stay
- A press and content moment you can amplify massively
- Proof of concept for permanent concession

**How to get in the door:**
- Research the specific Womenswear/Sportswear buyer by name (LinkedIn, industry press)
- Never email "Dear Buyer" — always address by name
- Attend Pure London or Moda where Selfridges buyers actively scout
- Attend Enterprise Nation pitch events where Selfridges buyers are panellists
- Bring community activation proposals: in-store wellness events, run club launches, Ruth-hosted sessions. Selfridges values brands that bring experiences, not just products.

**What Selfridges is looking for:**
- Innovation — "something you wouldn't regularly find"
- Sustainability — "well made, sustainably sourced, ethically produced"
- Community — brands that bring engaged audiences into the store
- Brand heat — sellout track record, press buzz, social proof
- Exclusivity — products or colourways they get first or exclusively

**If the pop-up succeeds:**
- Negotiate permanent concession space
- Propose seasonal community activations in-store
- Offer Selfridges 2-4 week exclusivity windows on new wholesale capsule drops
- Expand to Selfridges Manchester and Birmingham

**Objective:** Scale to a recognised luxury activewear brand across multiple prestigious retailers and territories.

**Retail expansion roadmap:**

| Timeline | Retailer | Territory | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 3 | Selfridges (permanent) | UK | Pop-up → concession → permanent space |
| Year 3-4 | Net-a-Porter | Global | Online luxury wholesale, exclusive colourways |

| Year 4 | Harrods | UK | Premium tier, potential in-store activation |
| Year 4-5 | Nordstrom | US | US market entry, leveraging Net-a-Porter data |
| Year 5 | Le Bon Marché | France | European market entry |

**Technology roadmap:**
- By Year 3: Infrared + silver ion standard across all products
- Year 3-4: Apply for Innovate UK Smart Grant (£100K-£500K) for quad-tech R&D
- Year 4-5: Introduce phase change thermoregulation (PCM) once grant-funded R&D solves the wash durability problem (currently dies at ~10-15 washes)
- Year 5+: Full quad-tech product line (infrared + silver + PCM + mineral microcapsules)

**Revenue model shift:**

| Year | DTC % | Wholesale % | Est. Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 100% | 0% | £80-200K |
| 2 | 100% | 0% | £400K-1M |
| 3 | 70% | 30% | £1-3M |
| 4 | 60% | 40% | £3-5M |
| 5 | 55-60% | 40-45% | £5-8M |

**Non-negotiable:** Wholesale should never exceed 40-45% of total revenue. DTC margins (75%) fund the business. Wholesale margins (48%) build prestige. If wholesale starts to dominate, you become dependent on retailer terms and lose pricing power.

**Brand milestones:**
- First standalone store (London, Year 4-5)
- B Corp certification (sustainability credibility)
- Independent clinical trials on the combined technology stack (marketing claim ammunition)
- International community: run clubs and wellness events in every city where the product is sold

## Brand Protection Rules

These rules govern every decision from Vault 001 through Year 5 and beyond:

1. **Never build inventory you haven't already sold.** Pre-orders and crowdfunding only for the first 3+ drops. No speculative manufacturing.

2. **Lawyer before launch.** Celebrity partnership contract must be locked before any public promotion. Sweat equity, vesting, deliverables, exit terms — all in writing.

3. **One product, one technology, one launch.** Start with one hero SKU and one technology per Vault. Stack complexity across drops, not within them.

4. **Audience before product.** 5,000+ waitlist before spending on production. Content-first, community-first, product comes after.

5. **Never make a health claim.** Describe the technology ("made with infrared bioceramic minerals"), never the outcome ("improves circulation"). ASA and DMCCA enforcement is real.

6. **Sample, wash, destroy — then order.** Wash-test 10+ times. Stretch-test under real conditions. If it's not perfect at wash 10, do not approve production.

7. **Protect the name before you say it out loud.** Trademark, domains, social handles — all registered same day.

8. **Budget for what you don't know.** Keep 20% of capital as untouchable contingency. Hire a freelance production consultant for the first run.

9. **Final sale on limited drops.** Returns kill margin at this scale. Compensate with obsessive size guides, fit videos, and measurement charts.

10. **Have a CELLIANT Plan B on day one.** The brand story is "infrared bioceramic technology," not "CELLIANT." The technology is bigger than any single licensor.

- **Technology stack** — no competitor combines infrared + silver ion (+ PCM in later years) in fashion activewear
- **Community** — Vault buyers become a self-reinforcing tribe. Founding members have identity and status.

- **Numbered scarcity** — creates collectibility and resale value that standard activewear can't replicate
- **Data advantage** — every Vault generates sizing, preference, and demand data that makes the next Vault more precise
