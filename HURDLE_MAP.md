# MaisonMove — Hurdle Map

## TIER 1: Business-Killing Hurdles

### 1. The Cash Flow Death Spiral

Manufacturers want 30-50% deposit before production and the balance before shipping. You pay in Month 1, goods arrive Month 3, you sell over Months 4-6, but returns eat into revenue for Months 5-7. You're always funding the next production run with money you haven't earned yet.

On $10-20K starting capital, you get one shot. If your first run doesn't sell through fast enough to fund run two, you're dead.

**Mitigation:** Pre-orders only. Never manufacture what isn't already paid for. Use crowdfunding to collect revenue before committing to production.

---

### 2. Returns Will Eat You Alive

Online fashion returns run 20-30%, and activewear sizing is notoriously inconsistent. Each return costs ~£8-15 in processing + shipping + repackaging. On a £80 legging with 65% gross margin, a return wipes out the profit on two additional sales.

The sizing problem is worse for a new brand — customers don't know your fit yet. "Bracketing" (ordering 2-3 sizes, returning what doesn't fit) is standard behaviour, especially with younger buyers.

At launch scale, a 25% return rate on a 200-unit launch = 50 units coming back. That's potentially £400-750 in dead logistics costs alone, plus units you may not be able to resell as new.

**Mitigation:**
- Obsess over a detailed size guide with actual body measurements
- Launch with 1-2 SKUs max (one legging, one bra) to keep sizing data tight
- Consider "final sale" on first drops (common in limited-run brands)
- Budget 15% of revenue for returns handling

---

### 3. CELLIANT May Not Want You (Yet)

CELLIANT explicitly says they're not a good fit for startups and small-run products. Their partnership process involves:
- Strategic fit evaluation
- Brand License Agreement
- Onboarding across product dev, supply chain, marketing, and regulatory teams
- Product certification before market entry

They don't publish timelines, but this reads like a 3-6 month process minimum with a real chance they say no to a zero-revenue startup with no manufacturing track record.

CELLIANT Print is the lowest barrier, but you'd still need their approval and a mill/printer partner they authorize.

**Mitigation:**
- Approach with celebrity backing + clear market positioning to stand out from typical cold startups
- Have brand, audience, and pre-order demand before approaching. Show 5,000+ waitlist emails
- Backup plan: Research non-CELLIANT bioceramic suppliers (Emana yarn by Rhodia, Baller Athletik's supplier network)

---

### 4. The Celebrity Partnership Structure

This relationship is the single biggest asset and the single biggest risk. Get the legal structure wrong and it can destroy the business.

| Structure | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Paid ambassador | Simple, low risk | Can't afford the rate — cash upfront kills runway |
| Equity co-founder | Aligned incentives, no cash outlay | May not want operational involvement. If relationship sours, they own part of the company |
| Sweat equity deal | Middle ground — equity vests based on deliverables | Requires extremely detailed contracts defining minimum participation, content obligations, step-up provisions. Needs entertainment lawyer (£2-5K) |

Any contracted deal should have the same detail as a traditional celebrity endorsement — every expectation in writing, minimum participation levels defined, clear exit terms.

**Mitigation:** Budget £2,000-5,000 for an entertainment/partnership lawyer. Non-negotiable spend. Structure as sweat equity with clear vesting + deliverable milestones. Get this done before any public promotion.

## TIER 2: Expensive & Painful Hurdles

### 5. Advertising Claims Regulation (ASA + DMCCA)

The UK's Advertising Standards Authority requires documentary evidence for all objective claims before publication. The Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 adds statutory teeth to misleading claims.

**What you cannot say** without independent clinical evidence specific to your product:
- "Improves circulation"
- "Boosts recovery"
- "Reduces cellulite"
- "Regulates body temperature" (unless you can prove duration + conditions)
- "Increases energy"

**What you can probably say:**
- "Made with CELLIANT infrared technology" (ingredient claim, not health claim)
- "Infused with bioceramic minerals"
- "Antimicrobial silver-ion treated" (as a material fact)

If CELLIANT is your partner, you can lean on their clinical studies — but the ASA may still want evidence that your specific product (not just the raw technology) delivers the claimed benefit.

**Mitigation:**
- Hire a regulatory copywriter (£500-1,000) to review all product descriptions and ad copy before launch
- Never make health claims. Describe the technology, let customers infer the benefits
- Use CELLIANT's own approved marketing language (they provide this to partners)

---

### 6. Quality Control on First Production Runs

Industry standard defect rates for activewear: 0% critical, 1.5-2.5% major, 4.0% minor. First-run reality with a new manufacturer? Often much worse.

**Common first-run disasters:**
- Size inconsistency from cutting/grading errors (your M fits like an L)
- Seam failures under stretch (leggings blow out mid-squat = social media nightmare)
- Colour variation between units in the same batch
- Fabric pilling after 2-3 washes
- CELLIANT Print adhesion issues — if the mineral print doesn't bond properly, it flakes or washes off before the claimed 50 washes

**Mitigation:**
- Order samples before bulk (always, non-negotiable)
- Wash-test samples 10+ times before approving production
- Use AQL inspection standards — even on a 50-unit run, inspect 20% randomly
- Budget for a small third-party QC inspection if manufacturing overseas (~£200-500 per inspection)

---

### 7. Textile Labelling Compliance

UK law (Textile Products (Labelling and Fibre Composition) Regulations 2012) requires:
- Fibre composition listed in descending order by weight percentage on a permanently attached label
- All fibre names must use standard recognised names (Polyester, Polyamide, Elastane — not "magic infrared yarn")
- Labels must be durable, securely attached, easily legible, in English
- If selling to EU: separate EU textile labelling requirements apply (Regulation EU 1007/2011)

CELLIANT's bioceramic minerals aren't a "fibre" — they're an additive. You'll need to label the base fibre correctly and can reference CELLIANT separately on hangtags/marketing, not on the composition label itself.

**Mitigation:** Your manufacturer should handle this, but verify. A labelling error can trigger Trading Standards enforcement. Budget £200-500 for a compliance consultant review if unsure.

## TIER 3: Annoying but Manageable Hurdles

### 8. The Monthly Burn Before Revenue

Even before selling anything, the clock is ticking:

| Recurring cost | Monthly |
|---|---|
| Shopify Basic | £24/mo (£1/mo first 3 months) |
| Domain | ~£1/mo |
| Email marketing (Mailchimp free to paid) | £0-30/mo |
| Shopify apps (reviews, size guide, etc.) | £20-50/mo |
| Accounting software | £12-30/mo |
| Product liability insurance | £6-30/mo |
| Storage (if self-fulfilling) | £0 (bedroom) to £100+ |

That's roughly £60-165/month in overhead before selling a single unit. Not fatal, but it compounds — 6 months of pre-revenue burn at £100/mo = £600 gone.

**Mitigation:** Keep Shopify at £1/mo during build phase. Use free tiers of everything. Self-fulfill from home until 50+ orders/month, then switch to 3PL (~£4.35/order).

---

### 9. Company Formation & IP Protection

- UK Ltd company: £50 (Companies House direct)
- UK trademark (one class): £170 (IPO direct, 10-year protection)
- You want Class 25 (clothing) and possibly Class 35 (retail services)
- Two classes = £220 total

**Timing trap:** Trademark registration takes ~4 months if unopposed. If someone is already using a similar name in activewear, you could face opposition. Search the UK IPO register before committing to a brand name.

Also register: Instagram handle, TikTok handle, .com and .co.uk domains — do this the same day you pick the name, before anyone can squat.

---

### 10. No Industry Experience

The biggest meta-hurdle: no industry experience means mistakes that experienced founders avoid instinctively:
- Not knowing which manufacturer red flags to watch for
- Not understanding fabric weight, GSM, stretch recovery, or how to read a tech pack
- Underestimating lead times (6-12 weeks is standard, not 2)
- Not realizing that "custom colour" adds 2-4 weeks and often a separate MOQ
- Ordering the wrong size ratio (too many XS, not enough L/XL)

**Mitigation:**
- Find a freelance fashion production consultant (£30-60/hr). Even 5-10 hours of guidance on the first production run could save thousands in mistakes
- Join UKFT (UK Fashion & Textile Association) — they offer labelling toolkits, export guides, and manufacturer connections
- Read every Starter Story and Indie Hackers case study on activewear brand launches

## Timeline: What Hits When

```

            Risk: Partnership deal falls apart without proper contract

Month 2-3:  CELLIANT outreach, manufacturer sourcing, samples
            Risk: CELLIANT says no, back to Plan B suppliers

Month 3-5:  Sample development, wash testing, QC
            Risk: Samples aren't right, 2-3 revision cycles eat time

Month 4-6:  Content creation, waitlist building, pre-launch
            Risk: Audience doesn't materialize, celebrity audience doesn't convert

Month 6-8:  Crowdfunding or pre-order launch, first production
            Risk: Campaign underperforms, can't fund MOQ

Month 8-10: Fulfillment, first customer feedback, returns handling
            Risk: Sizing issues, QC defects, negative reviews

Month 10-12: Second drop, cash flow management, scaling decisions
             Risk: Cash tied up in inventory, can't fund growth
```

---

## The Single Most Important Thing

Of everything above, the celebrity partnership legal structure is the one that, if fumbled, is completely unrecoverable. Every other hurdle can be solved with time, money, or pivoting. A botched celebrity partnership — especially with someone who's a personal connection — can destroy both the business and the relationship.

Get the lawyer. Get the contract. Get it done first.
