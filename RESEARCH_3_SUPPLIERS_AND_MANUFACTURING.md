# Maison Move Further Research: Suppliers & Manufacturing Guide

---

## 1. The Full Process (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Create Tech Packs
Before approaching any manufacturer, you need tech packs for each design. A tech pack includes:
- Flat sketches (front, back, side views)
- Measurements for every size
- Fabric specifications (weight, composition, finish)
- Construction details (seam types, stitch types)
- Trim details (labels, hang tags, elastics, zips)
- Colourways
- Compression zone mapping (for SculptMap pieces)

**How to get tech packs made:**
- Hire a freelance fashion designer on Upwork or People Per Hour
- Cost: £150-£400 per style
- Look for someone with activewear/sportswear experience specifically
- Timeline: 1-2 weeks per style

### Step 2: Research and Shortlist Manufacturers
Contact 3-5 manufacturers. Send them your tech packs and ask for:
- Sample pricing and lead time
- MOQs (minimum order quantities) for bulk production
- Fabric sourcing capabilities (can they source performance fabrics?)
- Certifications (Oeko-Tex, GRS, BSCI)
- References from other brands they've worked with

### Step 3: Order Development Samples (YES — Try on Yourself First)
**This is critical. Do NOT skip this step.**
- Order 1-2 samples per style from 2-3 different manufacturers
- Cost: £50-£200 per sample depending on complexity
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks per round
- **Try them on. Train in them. Wash them. Wear them all day.**
- Test compression, opacity (squat test), comfort, fabric feel, recovery
- Do at least 2 rounds of sampling before committing to bulk

### Step 4: Fit Testing
- Get 3-5 women across your size range to test the samples
- Ask for honest feedback on fit, comfort, compression, and style
- Adjust tech packs based on feedback
- Order revised samples if needed (Round 2)

### Step 5: Pre-Production Sample
- Once you approve the final sample, request a pre-production sample (PPS)
- This is the EXACT garment that will be produced in bulk
- Check every detail: stitching, labels, colour, measurements
- Only approve bulk production when the PPS is perfect

### Step 6: First Bulk Production Run
- Start small: 100-200 units per style across your size range
- This is your test run — enough to launch DTC and gather real customer data
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks after PPS approval + shipping

### Step 7: Quality Control
- Request photos and measurements of finished garments before shipping
- Consider hiring a third-party QC inspector (especially for international manufacturers)
- Cost for QC: £100-£300 per inspection visit

## 2. How to Approach a Manufacturer

### Your First Email Should Include:
1. **Brief brand introduction** — who you are, what Maison Move is, your aesthetic
2. **What you need** — how many styles, what type of garments
3. **Fabric requirements** — performance fabrics, certifications needed
4. **Order volume** — be honest about starting small
5. **Timeline** — when you need samples and when you plan to launch
6. **Tech packs** — attach them if you have them, or say "in development"

### Example Email Template:
```
Subject: New Brand Enquiry — Premium Women's Activewear

Hi [Name],

I'm Sapph, founder of Maison Move — a premium women's activewear and loungewear 
brand launching in the UK. We're positioned at the £65-£150 price point, combining 
performance fabric technology with fashion-forward design.

We're looking for a manufacturing partner for our debut capsule collection 
(8-12 styles) including seamless leggings, sports bras, and cut-and-sew 
loungewear pieces.

Key requirements:
- Nylon-spandex blends with peached/buttery finish (200-280 GSM)
- Multi-zone compression capability (seamless knit)
- Oeko-Tex 100 and/or GRS certification
- Anti-microbial fabric treatment capability
- Low MOQ for initial production (100-300 units per style)

Could you share:
1. Your MOQ and pricing structure for the above?
2. Sample lead time and cost?
3. Any brand references or portfolio I can review?

I'd love to arrange a call to discuss further.

Best regards,
Sapph
Founder, Maison Move
```

### Questions to Ask Every Manufacturer:
- What is your MOQ per style/per colour?
- What is your sample cost and timeline?
- Can you source performance fabrics or do I need to supply my own?
- Do you have experience with seamless knit construction?
- Can you apply functional treatments (anti-microbial, far-infrared)?
- What certifications do you hold?
- Can you share references from brands you've worked with?
- What is your defect rate?
- What are your payment terms? (Usually 30% deposit, 70% before shipping)

### Red Flags:
- Says "yes" to everything without asking questions about your product
- Refuses to provide a company address or factory registration
- No portfolio or brand references
- Chaotic sample process (late, wrong, excuses)
- Pushes you to order more than you need
- No certifications

- VAT: 20% (reclaimable if VAT registered)
- Packaging: £1-£3 per unit
- Labels/hangtags: £0.50-£1.50 per unit

## 5. First Order Strategy

### Recommended Approach
1. **Sampling Phase (Month 1-2):** Order 2-3 samples of each style from 2 different manufacturers. Budget: £500-£1,500
2. **Fit Testing (Month 2-3):** Test on yourself and 3-5 other women. Budget: minimal (product cost only)
3. **Revised Samples (Month 3-4):** Order corrected samples. Budget: £300-£800
4. **First Bulk Run (Month 4-6):** Order 100-200 units per style, 2-3 colourways. Budget: £5,000-£15,000

### How Many Pieces to Start With
- **Per style, per colour, per size:** 15-25 units
- **Across 5 sizes (XS-XL):** 75-125 units per style per colour
- **For 8 styles in 2 colourways:** 1,200-2,000 units total
- **This is enough to:** Launch your DTC website, test demand, identify bestsellers, and have stock for micro-influencer seeding

### Size Ratio (Recommended for First Run)
| Size | % of Order |
|---|---|
| XS | 10% |
| S | 25% |
| M | 30% |
| L | 25% |
| XL | 10% |

Adjust after your first sell-through data.

## 6. Where's the Cheapest (With Good Quality)?

**Best value for a startup:** China (specifically Haining and Shaoxing regions for activewear)
- Unit costs 40-60% lower than UK manufacturing
- Many factories already produce for Western premium brands
- Strong seamless knitting capabilities
- Established Oeko-Tex and GRS certification infrastructure

**But consider:**
- UK manufacturing for hero pieces or limited runs (marketing value of "Made in UK")
- Use CanvasWhisper or AuraeFit for your first 5-10 test samples (ultra-low MOQ)
- Then move to a China factory like China Fitness Clothing or Shaoxing Starke for bulk

**The sweet spot:** Sample in the UK (faster iteration, easier communication), manufacture in China (lower unit cost, scalable).
